## Slide 1 — Title
**CityPulse — the operating system for municipal infrastructure.**
- AI civic-infrastructure intelligence: digital twin · predictive failure · 311 · AI agents · emergency
  · capital/grants.
- Live demo: https://citypulse-beta.vercel.app
- Contact: ctmakc@gmail.com
- *Speaker note:* One line — "We help cities see and fix infrastructure before it fails, and fund the
  fix." Then go.

## Slide 2 — Problem
**Cities can't fix what they can't see — or fund.**
- ASCE 2025: U.S. infrastructure at best-ever **"C"**, yet a **$3.7T** investment gap through 2033 (up
  from $2.59T).
- Roads **D+**, wastewater **D+**, stormwater **D**; wastewater+stormwater = **~$690B** 10-yr gap.
- Even with federal money, only **~30%** of water capital needs are being met.
- ~19,000 U.S. (+~3,700 CA) municipalities still run critical assets on **spreadsheets + reactive
  maintenance**.
- *Speaker note:* The gap isn't only dollars — it's visibility and prioritization. Cities find out
  after the flood.

## Slide 3 — Why now
**Historic money, brutal scrutiny, no time.**
- U.S. state/local infra spend hit **~$463B in 2024** — a 20-year high — but funding is constrained and
  increasingly tied to **documented need + resilience outcomes**.
- Key federal authorizations approach expiry; competition for dollars is intensifying.
- Enabling tech matured: digital-twin market **~$33B (2025) → ~$49B+ (2030)**; smart cities **~$700B →
  ~$1.45T**.
- Cities that can **quantify risk and prove resilience** win funding; the rest fall behind.
- *Speaker note:* This is the rare moment when the buyer has both the mandate and the money — and a
  reason to move now.

## Slide 4 — Solution + Live demo
**One platform. The whole city, live and predictive.**
- Six capabilities, one system: **digital twin · predictive failure · 311 · AI agents · emergency ·
  capital/grants.**
- **This is live software, not slides:** open https://citypulse-beta.vercel.app on screen.
- Production-grade: **Next.js + NestJS + PostGIS + Redis**, **WCAG 2.1 AA**, tested, CI, Docker.
- *Speaker note:* Drive the demo here for 60–90s — map → a predicted failure with lead time → a
  311 auto-route → the grant view. Let it breathe.

## Slide 5 — Product
**From raw signals to ranked, explainable action.**
- **Digital twin** of assets/networks on an interactive PostGIS map.
- **Predictive failure** scoring with lead time, reactive → pre-emptive.
- **311 intake** that auto-classifies, geolocates, and routes to the right crew.
- **Emergency mode** for coordinated incident response.
- **Capital & grant view** linking documented risk to fundable projects.
- *Speaker note:* Everything funnels to one accessible dashboard a public-works team can actually use.

## Slide 6 — AI agents
**Agents that do the triage humans don't have time for.**
- Triage incoming signals and citizen reports; cut the noise.
- Summarize asset/area conditions into plain-language briefings.
- Draft work orders and recommend the **next best action** — with explanations staff can trust.
- Map a city's risk to the **specific grant programs** that fund the fix.
- Humans stay in control: agents recommend, staff approve and act.
- *Speaker note:* This is the leverage — one operator supervising the city instead of chasing it.

## Slide 8 — Market
**A ~$463B/yr public market with a $3.7T gap.**
- **U.S.:** ~$463B state/local infra spend (2024); $3.7T gap; ~19,000 municipalities.
- **Canada:** ~$270B municipal deficit; locals own **>60%** of core infra on **8–10¢/tax-dollar**;
  water/wastewater alone needs **>$50B**; Build Communities Strong Fund = **$51B/10 yrs**.
- Every municipality is a buyer with a legal mandate to keep assets alive, documented, and funded.
- *Speaker note:* TAM is the whole tier of government that owns the pipes and roads — not a niche.

## Slide 9 — Business model
**Tiered SaaS built for procurement — that helps cities fund itself.**
- **Pilot → City → Region → Enterprise**, priced to population + infrastructure footprint.
- Paid pilot lands fast and de-risks the buy; annual subscriptions + departmental/regional expansion.
- Grant-intelligence layer can help a city **fund its CityPulse subscription** from the capital it
  surfaces.
- *Speaker note:* Land-and-expand, with a built-in answer to "how do we pay for this?"

## Slide 10 — Go-to-market
**Win one city, prove it, expand by department and region.**
- Wedge: a single high-pain department (water/stormwater) via a **paid pilot** with measured outcomes.
- Convert pilot → annual contract; expand to roads, emergency, and adjacent departments.
- Scale via regional/county scope, **state procurement vehicles**, and resilience/grant-funded entry.
- Public-benefit track for small/rural/disadvantaged communities widens reach and strengthens grants.
- *Speaker note:* Reference: see GTM-ROADMAP for the credibility/lead/discoverability layers already
  built around the demo.
