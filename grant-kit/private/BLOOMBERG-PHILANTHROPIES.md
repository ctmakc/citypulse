## 1. Why Bloomberg (alignment to published priorities)

Bloomberg Philanthropies creates lasting change across five areas — **the Arts, Education, Environment,
Public Health, and Government Innovation.** Its Government Innovation portfolio (the **Mayors
Challenge**, **What Works Cities**, and the **Bloomberg Harvard City Leadership Initiative**) is built
on one conviction: **cities run better when they use data and resident input to make decisions.**

The 2025–2026 Mayors Challenge drew **630+ applicant cities**; its **24 winners** (each receiving
**$1M** plus expert support) were explicitly recognized for projects that **upgrade core services using
AI and resident input.** What Works Cities Certification is the **international standard of data
excellence in city governance**, scoring cities against **43 criteria** for the practices, policies,
and *infrastructure* needed to harness data for better decisions.

CityPulse is purpose-built to be the **infrastructure those 43 criteria describe** and the **AI +
resident-input engine** the Mayors Challenge winners are praised for. It is the operational layer that
turns "we should be data-driven" into a running system.

## 2. The gap Bloomberg's programs keep hitting

Bloomberg's cities work has proven the *thesis* — data-driven, resident-informed government produces
better outcomes. The recurring constraint is **execution infrastructure**: most cities, especially the
30,000–500,000-population cities eligible for What Works Cities, **lack a single system that unifies
asset data, resident input, and AI decision support.** They have:

- Data scattered across spreadsheets, paper logs, and disconnected departmental tools.
- Resident input (311) that arrives but doesn't *route*, *measure*, or *close the loop*.
- No predictive layer, so "use data to decide" still means reacting after something breaks.
- No way to **demonstrate** the data practices certification requires, even when the will exists.

A city can have an ambitious mayor and a Bloomberg cohort seat and *still* stall — because there is no
deployable platform underneath. CityPulse is that platform.

## 3. What CityPulse is

**CityPulse is the operating system for municipal infrastructure** — a live, predictive, AI-native view
of every asset a city owns, fused with the resident-input channel that keeps it honest:

- **Digital twin** of city assets and networks on an interactive map.
- **Predictive asset-failure intelligence** — failures flagged *before* they happen, with lead time and
  explainable risk.
- **311 / citizen-request intake** — auto-classifies, geolocates, routes, and **closes the loop** with
  residents (the "resident input" Bloomberg prizes, instrumented and measurable).
- **AI agents** that triage signals and surface the next best action — the "AI to improve core
  services" the Mayors Challenge winners demonstrate.
- **Emergency / incident mode** for coordinated multi-agency response.
- **Capital & grant intelligence** mapping asset risk to funding programs.

Real and online today: a **live, working demo** at https://citypulse-beta.vercel.app. Built
production-grade (Next.js + NestJS + PostGIS + Redis), **WCAG 2.1 AA** accessible, with tests, CI, and
Docker deployment.

## 4. How CityPulse maps to What Works Cities Certification

CityPulse directly produces evidence against the certification's data-practice criteria:

| Certification theme | What CityPulse provides |
|---|---|
| **Data governance & infrastructure** | A unified spatial data platform (PostGIS) of record for city assets, 311, and environmental data |
| **Open data & transparency** | Resident-facing portal + open civic-data feed (configurable) |
| **Performance & analytics** | Live dashboards, KPIs, predictive risk scoring, audit trail of every action |
| **Repurposing data for decisions** | AI agents that convert raw data into ranked, explainable recommendations |
| **Resident engagement / input** | Closed-loop 311 with tracking codes and neighborhood-level response metrics |
| **Results-driven contracting / capital** | Risk → cost → funding-source pipeline in the capital/grant module |

This turns certification from a paperwork exercise into a **byproduct of running the city on CityPulse.**
