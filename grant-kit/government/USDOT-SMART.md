## ⚠️ Program-status note (READ FIRST)

The named program — **USDOT SMART (Strengthening Mobility and Revolutionizing Transportation)** — has **materially changed and is effectively wound down for new applicants**:

- **No new SMART notices of funding will be issued.** Congress passed **H.R. 7148, the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2026**, which **rescinds ~$204.9M in unobligated SMART balances.**
- Existing agreements (**122 Stage 1** and **7+ Stage 2** grants) **will be honored**, but **Stage 2 Implementation is open only to prior Stage 1 recipients** — so SMART is not a viable new-entry path for CityPulse.

**Therefore this narrative is re-targeted to the closest CURRENT equivalents** that fund exactly what SMART funded (data-driven, technology-forward transportation), where a city/county can apply now:

| Program | Status (May 2026) | Why it's the closest fit |
|---|---|---|
| **SS4A — Safe Streets and Roads for All** | **ACTIVE & RECURRING.** FY2026 NOFO closed **May 26, 2026**; **~$1B still available for the next round** — a near-term recurring opportunity. | Funds **data-driven safety planning, demonstration, and implementation** — CityPulse's predictive analytics + 311 fit Planning/Demonstration grants directly. Cities, counties, MPOs, Tribes apply **directly**. |
| **PROTECT (discretionary)** | **Resilience program; NOFO currently removed (since Feb 11, 2025) — monitor for reopening.** Prior cycle Oct 2024–Feb 2025. | Funds **surface-transportation resilience** to flooding, extreme weather, climate — CityPulse's hazard/resilience engine fits the **planning** and **resilience-improvement** grant types. |

**Primary target: SS4A** (live, recurring, direct city eligibility). PROTECT is the secondary/resilience target when its NOFO reopens. The narrative below is written for **SS4A** with PROTECT framing called out.

## 1. Problem Statement

Roadway-safety and transportation-resilience decisions are still made with **lagging, fragmented data**. Cities and counties:

- Plan safety interventions from **historical crash reports** that arrive months late and miss near-misses, hazardous conditions, and resident-reported danger spots entirely.
- Lack a **unified, equity-aware view** of where the most dangerous and most inequitably burdened corridors are — so safety dollars don't reliably reach the neighborhoods with the highest fatal/serious-injury risk.
- Treat **transportation resilience** (flood-prone underpasses, evacuation routes, climate-vulnerable corridors) as separate from safety, when both depend on the same asset and condition intelligence.
- Struggle to produce the **Comprehensive Safety Action Plan** SS4A requires, because the underlying data is scattered across agencies and not analysis-ready.

The result: predictable crashes and closures, inequitable safety outcomes, and transportation systems that fail in the exact extreme-weather events that are becoming more frequent.

## 2. Project Description

**CityPulse** is an AI civic-infrastructure platform. For an SS4A-funded effort it delivers:

1. **Data-driven safety analytics** — fuses crash data, roadway/asset condition, speed and volume data, and **311 citizen reports** to identify and rank high-risk corridors and intersections (including systemic, predictive risk, not just where crashes already happened).
2. **Equity-aware prioritization** — weights safety projects by social vulnerability and exposure so interventions reach the communities bearing the greatest safety burden — a core SS4A objective.
3. **Citizen 311 reporting** — residents report dangerous conditions (failing signals, flooded underpasses, dark crossings, near-misses), feeding ground-truth into the safety model and demonstrating community engagement.
4. **Resilience overlay (PROTECT-aligned)** — flags climate-vulnerable corridors, flood-prone segments, and evacuation routes for resilience investment.
5. **Action-Plan engine** — assembles the analysis, equity assessment, and prioritized project list into the structure of a **Comprehensive Safety Action Plan**, and supports the **demonstration** component with live data.

**Live demo:** https://citypulse-beta.vercel.app
**Illustrative impact (pilot-modeled):** **82% of infrastructure failures pre-detected** and **$4.2M/yr in avoided cost** — directly relevant to preventing dangerous failures and demonstrating measurable safety/efficiency outcomes.

## 4. Public Benefit

- **Fewer fatalities and serious injuries** through proactive, data-driven safety targeting (toward zero traffic deaths).
- **More equitable streets**: safety investment reaches historically underserved neighborhoods.
- **More resilient mobility**: climate-vulnerable corridors and evacuation routes are identified and hardened.
- **Public savings + transparency**: better-targeted projects and an auditable, resident-engaged decision process.

## 5. Climate & Equity Framing

CityPulse unifies safety and **climate resilience**: the same platform that ranks dangerous corridors also flags flood-prone and climate-vulnerable segments and evacuation routes, supporting adaptation of the transportation network (the PROTECT mission). Its equity engine ensures federal safety and resilience dollars reach the communities most exposed to both crash risk and climate hazard — aligning with SS4A's equity emphasis and Justice40-style distributional goals.

## 9. Sustainability

CityPulse persists as a multi-year municipal subscription with a defined business model. SS4A funds the **planning, demonstration, integration, and engagement**; the city/county sustains the platform from its operating budget — justified by avoided costs and by the safety value of an always-current Action Plan (SS4A requires plans be kept up to date). The platform then de-risks and accelerates a future SS4A **Implementation** grant, and the same data backbone supports PROTECT resilience applications — compounding the value of the initial federal investment.
