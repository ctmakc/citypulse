## 1. Problem Statement

Canadian municipalities own the majority of the country's public infrastructure yet face climate impacts — flooding, freeze-thaw, extreme heat, wildfire smoke, and intensifying storms — with limited capacity to **anticipate which assets are most at risk and act before they fail.** Specifically:

- Adaptation planning is often **document-based and static**, disconnected from the live condition and climate exposure of culverts, stormwater systems, roads, and water assets.
- Municipalities cannot easily answer *"Which public assets are most likely to fail under future climate conditions, and which failures would most harm vulnerable residents?"*
- Smaller and northern communities have the **least engineering capacity** but the **highest relative exposure**, so risk goes unmeasured until an asset fails.
- Adaptation investment is hard to prioritize and even harder to **prove** — making it difficult to justify spending and to report nature-positive, risk-reduction outcomes.

The missing layer is a **live, equity-aware climate-risk and asset-intelligence system** that turns adaptation from a plan on a shelf into a continuously managed pipeline of actions.

## 2. Project Description

**CityPulse** is an AI civic-infrastructure platform. For a GMF Adaptation in Action project it delivers:

1. **Climate-risk modeling for public infrastructure** — fuses asset inventories, inspection/condition data, 311 reports, and climate-hazard layers (flood, heat, freeze-thaw, storm) to forecast which public assets are most at risk under current and future climate conditions.
2. **Equity-aware adaptation prioritization** — ranks adaptation actions by both infrastructure risk and impact on vulnerable populations, so investment reduces risk where residents are least able to cope.
3. **Predictive maintenance** — identifies failures before they happen, protecting public infrastructure and avoiding costly emergency repairs.
4. **Citizen 311 channel** — residents report local climate impacts (flooding, drainage failures, heat-vulnerable sites), feeding ground-truth into the adaptation model.
5. **Outcome tracking** — measures risk reduction, avoided cost, and (paired with green-infrastructure measures) nature-positive outcomes for FCM reporting.

**Live demo:** https://citypulse-beta.vercel.app
**Illustrative impact (pilot-modeled):** **$4.2M/yr in avoided cost** and **82% of failures pre-detected** before service interruption — the risk-reduction and protect-public-infrastructure evidence GMF wants.

## 3. How CityPulse Meets GMF's Stated Goals

| GMF / Adaptation in Action objective | How CityPulse responds |
|---|---|
| **Reduce climate risks** | Forecasts and ranks climate risk to public assets, driving targeted risk-reduction action. |
| **Protect public infrastructure** | Predictive maintenance + hazard overlay protect the culverts, stormwater, roads, and water assets municipalities own. |
| **Deliver nature-positive solutions** | Identifies where green/nature-based adaptation (bioswales, naturalized stormwater, urban canopy for heat) yields the most benefit, and tracks outcomes. |
| **Help all communities, incl. small / northern / Indigenous-partnered** | Equity-aware prioritization + low-capacity-friendly deployment serve communities with the least engineering capacity. |
| **Demonstrate measurable results** | Built-in outcome tracking produces the risk-reduction and avoided-cost evidence FCM requires for reporting. |
| **Build municipal capacity** | Trains staff to own the adaptation pipeline, leaving enduring in-house capability. |

## 4. Public Benefit

- **Protected public infrastructure** and fewer climate-driven failures and service disruptions.
- **Safer, more resilient communities**, with priority to vulnerable and under-resourced residents.
- **Taxpayer savings** through avoided emergency repairs and better-targeted adaptation spending.
- **Transparent, evidence-based decisions** that residents and councils can see and trust.

## 5. Climate & Equity Framing

CityPulse is a **pure climate-adaptation instrument**: it exists to help communities anticipate and reduce climate risk to the infrastructure people depend on. Its equity engine ensures adaptation benefits reach vulnerable, low-income, northern, and Indigenous-partnered communities — consistent with GMF's commitment to help **all** communities adapt and with Canada's broader climate-equity goals. By identifying where **nature-based solutions** deliver the most resilience, it also advances GMF's nature-positive priority.

## 6. Work Plan (Implementation Project — up to 24 months)

| Phase | Months | Activities | Deliverables |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Data integration & climate-risk baseline** | 1–6 | Ingest asset, condition, 311, and climate-hazard data; stand up secure tenant; build risk models. | Climate-risk baseline; data governance docs. |
| **2. Prioritized adaptation pipeline** | 4–10 | Generate ranked, equity-weighted adaptation actions; co-develop with municipal engineers; pair with nature-positive measures. | Prioritized adaptation plan; nature-based options. |
| **3. Implement priority actions** | 8–18 | Implement top adaptation measures (incl. green infrastructure where prioritized); deploy predictive maintenance. | Implemented adaptation actions; live monitoring. |
| **4. Citizen channel + monitoring** | 10–20 | Launch 311 reporting; close the loop into the risk model. | Live citizen channel; updated risk model. |
| **5. Evaluation & sustainment** | 18–24 | Measure risk reduction, avoided cost, nature-positive outcomes; train staff; transition to operations. | FCM outcome report; sustainment plan. |

## 9. Sustainability

CityPulse persists as a multi-year municipal subscription with a defined Canadian municipal business model (Pilot/City/Region tiers). GMF funds the **implementation, integration, and capacity building**; the municipality sustains the platform from its operating budget — justified by documented avoided costs and by the ongoing value of a live adaptation pipeline. Staff are trained to own the system, outputs are embedded in the municipality's asset-management and climate-adaptation plans, and the capability endures across council terms — exactly the lasting local capacity GMF aims to build.
