## 1. Problem Statement

Local governments face escalating, compounding natural-hazard risk — riverine and flash flooding, extreme heat, wind, and aging-infrastructure failure — while operating with mitigation budgets that are a fraction of replacement need. The core failure is **not** a lack of capital alone; it is a **lack of risk visibility**:

- Mitigation dollars are allocated **reactively**, after failures and disaster declarations, rather than to the assets most likely to fail and cause cascading lifeline loss.
- Most jurisdictions cannot answer, with evidence, *"Which of our culverts, pump stations, bridges, and stormwater assets are most likely to fail in the next hazard event, and which failures would harm the most vulnerable residents?"*
- Hazard Mitigation Plans are often static documents, refreshed every five years, disconnected from the live condition and performance of the assets they cover.
- Benefit-Cost Analysis (BCA) — BRIC's gatekeeper — is laborious precisely because jurisdictions lack the structured asset, condition, and avoided-loss data that a good BCA requires.

The result is a self-reinforcing cycle: under-targeted mitigation, predictable failures, costly post-disaster recovery, and inequitable outcomes for low-income and historically underserved neighborhoods that sit in the highest-hazard areas.

## 2. Project Description

**CityPulse** is an AI civic-infrastructure platform that turns a municipality's existing asset, sensor, work-order, and hazard data into a **live, prioritized mitigation pipeline**. For a BRIC-funded effort, CityPulse delivers four capabilities:

1. **Predictive maintenance & failure modeling** — ingests asset inventories, inspection histories, 311 reports, and available sensor feeds to forecast failure probability and remaining useful life for critical infrastructure (stormwater, culverts, pump stations, bridges, water/sewer).
2. **Climate & hazard-resilience overlay** — fuses asset risk with flood zones, heat exposure, and extreme-weather projections to identify which assets are both *likely to fail* and *exposed to hazard* — the intersection that BRIC funds.
3. **Equity-aware prioritization** — weights candidate mitigation projects by social-vulnerability and community-lifeline impact, so investment is steered toward disadvantaged and overburdened communities (directly supporting BRIC's equity priority and Justice40-aligned objectives).
4. **Citizen 311 + lived-experience signal** — a public reporting channel that surfaces ground-truth conditions (street flooding, drainage failures, heat-vulnerable sites) and feeds them back into the risk model and the Hazard Mitigation Plan.

**Live demo:** https://citypulse-beta.vercel.app — evaluators and the SHMO can see the working platform before award.

**Illustrative impact (pilot-modeled):** **$4.2M in avoided costs per year** and **82% of failures pre-detected** before service interruption in the modeled deployment — the exact avoided-loss and reliability evidence that strengthens a BRIC Benefit-Cost Analysis.

## 3. How CityPulse Meets BRIC's Stated Goals

| BRIC priority / evaluation criterion | How CityPulse responds |
|---|---|
| **Incentivize public infrastructure resilience projects** | Identifies, scopes, and ranks the specific physical mitigation projects (culvert upsizing, pump-station hardening, asset relocation) most worth funding — converting a vague need into BCA-ready project candidates. |
| **Mitigate risk to community lifelines** | Models cascading-failure and lifeline impact (water, transportation, safety) so mitigation protects the systems residents depend on in a disaster. |
| **Promote equity / benefit disadvantaged communities** | Equity-aware prioritization explicitly steers mitigation benefit to socially vulnerable and historically underserved areas; produces the distributional evidence reviewers score. |
| **Support adoption of building codes & standards** | Surfaces where code-plus-up investment and enforcement would most reduce risk; aligns with the Building Code Plus-Up set-aside. |
| **Strengthen Benefit-Cost Analysis & project readiness** | Generates structured asset, condition, and avoided-loss data that materially shortens and strengthens FEMA-compliant BCA — a frequent reason subapplications stall. |
| **Capability & Capacity Building (C&CB)** | CityPulse *is* a C&CB activity: risk assessment, mitigation planning, and project scoping that build a jurisdiction's enduring ability to identify and prioritize mitigation. |

## 4. Public Benefit

- **Lives and property protected** through pre-disaster mitigation rather than post-disaster recovery.
- **Taxpayer savings**: every dollar of mitigation returns multiples in avoided disaster losses; CityPulse increases that ratio by aiming dollars at the highest-risk, highest-impact assets.
- **Faster, defensible decisions**: elected officials and SHMOs get an auditable, evidence-based mitigation pipeline.
- **Resilient lifelines**: protection of the water, transportation, and safety systems that whole communities rely on.

## 5. Climate & Equity Framing

CityPulse operationalizes climate adaptation at the asset level: it ties each mitigation candidate to forward-looking hazard exposure (flood, heat, extreme weather), so the jurisdiction adapts *before* the next event. Its equity engine ensures that the benefits of federal mitigation funding reach the disadvantaged and overburdened communities BRIC prioritizes — producing the social-vulnerability and benefit-distribution evidence FEMA reviewers expect, and aligning with Justice40-style distributional goals.

## 6. Work Plan (24-month period of performance)

| Phase | Months | Activities | Deliverables |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Data integration & risk baseline** | 1–6 | Ingest asset, inspection, 311, sensor, and hazard data; stand up secure tenant; build failure + hazard models. | Live risk baseline; data governance + security documentation. |
| **2. Prioritized mitigation pipeline** | 4–10 | Generate ranked, equity-weighted mitigation candidates; co-develop with SHMO and local engineers. | Ranked project list; lifeline-impact + equity analysis. |
| **3. BCA acceleration & plan integration** | 8–16 | Produce structured avoided-loss data for FEMA BCA on top candidates; integrate outputs into the Hazard Mitigation Plan. | BCA-ready datasets; HMP update package. |
| **4. Citizen signal + monitoring** | 10–20 | Launch public 311/hazard reporting; close the loop into the risk model. | Live citizen channel; updated risk model. |
| **5. Evaluation & sustainment** | 18–24 | Measure detection rate, avoided-cost, equity distribution; train staff; transition to operations. | Outcome report; sustainment + staffing plan. |

## 9. Sustainability

CityPulse is delivered as a multi-year subscription with a defined post-grant business model (municipal Pilot/City/Region tiers), so the capability persists after federal funds end. The grant funds **integration, calibration, and capacity building**; the jurisdiction sustains the platform from its operating budget — justified by the documented avoided-cost (the same avoided-loss evidence used in the BCA). Staff are trained to own the risk pipeline, and outputs are embedded in the statutory Hazard Mitigation Plan, ensuring the investment endures across plan cycles and administrations.
