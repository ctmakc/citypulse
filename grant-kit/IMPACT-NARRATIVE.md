## 1. The public-benefit problem

Public infrastructure is a public good that is failing in slow motion. The 2025 ASCE Infrastructure
Report Card gave U.S. infrastructure its highest grade ever — a **"C"** — while projecting a **$3.7
trillion** investment gap through 2033. Roads sit at **D+**, wastewater at **D+**, stormwater at **D**;
wastewater and stormwater together carry an estimated **$690 billion** 10-year funding gap, and the
nation is meeting only about **30%** of water-system capital needs even with historic federal spending.

The harm of this gap is not abstract. When a stormwater culvert fails, neighborhoods flood. When a
sewer main backs up, untreated wastewater reaches homes and waterways. When a water main breaks, a
hospital or school can lose service. These failures land hardest on the residents least able to absorb
them. And the cities responsible — the ~19,000 U.S. municipalities and ~3,700 in Canada that own this
infrastructure — overwhelmingly manage it **reactively**, learning of failures only after service is
lost.

**CityPulse's public benefit is direct:** by making infrastructure visible and predictable *before* it
fails, the platform reduces service disruptions, environmental harm, and emergency cost — and channels
scarce public dollars to the highest-risk assets first.

## 2. Climate-resilience story

Climate change is converting routine infrastructure stress into recurring crisis. More intense
rainfall overwhelms stormwater systems graded **D**; heat and freeze-thaw cycles accelerate road and
pipe degradation; and the frequency of billion-dollar weather events keeps rising. Resilience funding
is flowing in response, but it is increasingly **conditioned on documented risk and measurable
resilience outcomes** — exactly the evidence most cities cannot produce.

CityPulse is built for this moment:

- **Predict before the storm.** Predictive asset-failure intelligence flags vulnerable stormwater and
  water assets ahead of failure, giving crews lead time to pre-position and prevent flooding and
  overflows rather than clean them up.
- **Coordinate during the event.** Emergency/incident mode gives staff shared situational awareness
  when conditions turn critical, shortening response and reducing harm.
- **Prove resilience after.** The digital twin and event history produce the **documented,
  spatially-explicit risk record** that resilience grant programs (e.g., hazard-mitigation and water-
  resilience funding) increasingly require — turning a city's lived experience into fundable,
  defensible projects.

The result is a measurable shift from **reactive disaster response to proactive resilience** — the
explicit goal of modern infrastructure-resilience policy.

## 3. Equity story

Federal and provincial funding for infrastructure exists — but the communities that need it most are
the least able to capture it. The U.S. EPA states plainly that disadvantaged and underserved
communities "often struggle to access federal funding" because of **limited expertise, difficulty
procuring engineers, geographic isolation, competing priorities, and the sheer complexity of the
application process** — which is why the EPA funds dedicated technical assistance (tens of millions of
dollars: e.g., **$49M** for rural/small/Tribal wastewater capacity, **$25M** FY25 for the Small,
Underserved, and Disadvantaged Communities program) just to help these communities **navigate** the
funding they're entitled to. In Canada, local governments own **more than 60%** of core public
infrastructure but receive only **8–10 cents of every tax dollar**, leaving small and rural
municipalities structurally under-resourced.

This is precisely the gap CityPulse's **capital & grant-intelligence layer** closes — at software cost,
at scale. By automatically mapping a community's documented asset risk to the specific programs that
fund it, and by drafting the evidence those applications need, CityPulse gives an under-resourced town
the same fundability as a well-staffed city.

**Our equity commitment is concrete:** a dedicated **public-benefit pricing track** for small, rural,
and disadvantaged communities so that predictive infrastructure intelligence — and the funding access
it unlocks — is not reserved for cities that can already afford it. Impact is tracked by the count and
profile of under-resourced communities served and the funding they successfully access.

## 4. Metrics and how they are measured

All headline figures are **illustrative reference-deployment ("Meridian") values**; a pilot replaces
them with the participating city's own measured results. CityPulse is instrumented to capture each one
from day one.

| Metric | Illustrative value | How it is measured |
|---|---|---|
| **Avoided cost / year** | **$4.2M** | Modeled avoided cost = (failures pre-detected × avg. emergency-vs-planned repair delta) + reduced overtime + extended asset life, using the city's own unit costs and asset inventory. Baseline = prior reactive spend. |
| **Failures pre-detected** | **82%** | Share of asset failures (or near-failures) that CityPulse flagged before service impact, over total failures in the period. Validated against the city's work-order / incident records. |
| **Predictive lead time** | **9 min (avg.)** | Time between CityPulse alert and the failure/threshold event on time-critical, instrumented assets. Slow-degrading assets are tracked separately on a condition-based (days–months) horizon. Lead time is sensor/event-class dependent. |
| **311 requests auto-routed** | **47** | Count of citizen requests auto-classified, geolocated, and routed to the correct crew without manual triage; accuracy validated against crew confirmation. |
| **Grant pipeline surfaced** | **$38.6M** | Total value of capital/grant programs matched to the city's documented asset risk by the grant-intelligence layer. Converts to a realized-funding metric once applications are submitted/awarded. |

**Measurement principles (so reviewers can trust the numbers):**
- **Baseline first.** Each pilot establishes the city's reactive-era baseline before attributing impact.
- **City's own data.** Costs, assets, and outcomes use the municipality's records, not generic averages.
- **Conservative attribution.** Avoided cost counts only events CityPulse actually flagged; grant value
  separates *surfaced* pipeline from *realized* awards.
- **Auditable.** The digital twin, alert log, and 311 routing history provide a reviewable trail.

## 5. Why this is fundable now

Public infrastructure money is flowing — U.S. state and local infrastructure spending reached roughly
**$463 billion in 2024**, a 20-year high — but it is constrained, scrutinized, and tied to documented
need and resilience outcomes. CityPulse produces exactly that evidence, for exactly the communities
funders are trying to reach. A grant in CityPulse is **catalytic**: it funds pilots that generate the
risk documentation and grant applications that unlock far larger downstream public-infrastructure
investment — with a deliberate focus on the small, rural, and disadvantaged communities that the
funding system, by its own admission, currently leaves behind.
